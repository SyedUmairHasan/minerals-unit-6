What is a Mineral?
SECTION 01 · FUNDAMENTALS
DEFINITION

A mineral is a naturally occurring, inorganic solid with a definite chemical formula, made of atoms/molecules arranged in a pattern (usually crystals). Minerals are non-renewable — they took millions of years to form.

The 5-Point Test (Geologists' Test)

For something to be classified as a mineral, it must pass ALL five of the following conditions:

1. Made Naturally

Formed through geological processes — volcanic action, heat, or pressure. Not man-made.

2. Inorganic

Not from a living thing. It is not biological in origin.

3. Solid

Must be in a solid physical state.

4. Chemical Formula

Has a definite and specific chemical composition.

5. Crystal Structure

Made up of atoms/molecules organised in a regular pattern — usually crystals.

📌
Key Fact

Minerals can be metallic (e.g. iron, copper, gold) or non-metallic (e.g. salt, gypsum, limestone). Both types are non-renewable resources.

Metal Ores

Most metals are found in ores — rocks made up of minerals. They are extracted by mining or quarrying. Common metal ores include:

Metallic Minerals in Pakistan
SECTION 02 · COPPER, GOLD & MORE

Pakistan has enormous deposits of many different minerals and natural resources — including metallic and non-metallic minerals, oil, natural gas, and gemstones.

Copper

Both copper and gold are found at Reko Diq mine in Chagai District, Balochistan — one of the world's largest copper deposits. The copper belt stretches from central/south-east Europe, Turkey, Iran, Pakistan, through the Himalayas into Myanmar, Malaysia, Indonesia, and Papua New Guinea.

📍
Copper deposits in Pakistan

Daht-e-Kuhn, Nokundi, and Saindak in Chagai District. Copper ore is dug from open pits and trucks take it away for extraction.

Gold

Pakistan has significant gold deposits worth billions of dollars. Main locations:

  • Rocks of Kirana Group, Chagai District (Saindak, Reko Diq)
  • Makran Area, Siahan Shale, Shardi, Shontar Nala (along Neelum river)
  • Krinji Formation, Sewakht Formation, tributaries of Indus and Chitral rivers
  • Jijal Ultramafic complex; various locations in Gilgit-Baltistan
🌍
Global Gold Fact

The United States holds the largest stockpile of gold in the world — over 8,100 tonnes of reserves.

🎯
EXAM TIP — GDP vs GNP

GDP (Gross Domestic Product) measures productivity within a country's geographical boundaries. GNP (Gross National Product) records economic activity by a country's citizens and businesses, even outside their borders. Mining contributes to both.

Cement

Made mainly from limestone, clay, and gypsum. Used as a binder in concrete for construction. Pakistan has 29 cement industries and exports cement to multiple countries. China produces the most cement globally, followed by India.

Salt — Khewra Salt Mines

The Khewra Salt Mines (Punjab) are among the world's oldest salt mines — discovered in 326 BCE when Alexander the Great was fighting Raja Porus. They cover an underground area of ~110 km² with about 6.5 billion tonnes of salt deposits.

Other salt mines: Warchi, Kalabagh (on Indus River banks), Jatta (near Karak), and Bahadur Khel.

Gemstone Mining in Pakistan

Pakistan is rich in high-quality gemstones — 30% of the world's gemstone deposits are in northern Pakistan and nearby Afghanistan. Pakistan ranks 4th or 5th in the world for the value of its gemstone deposits.

Main gemstone regions: mountains of Balochistan, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, and Gilgit-Baltistan. Peshawar is Pakistan's main centre for gemstone trading, with gem bazaars in Quetta, Lahore, and Islamabad.

Challenges of Gemstone Mining in Pakistan
  • Ground is frozen for most of the year; mining season only June/July to September/October
  • Mines are in steep, remote mountains with cliffs up to 1,200 metres and higher
  • Equipment must be carried by horses, donkeys, or miners themselves
  • Low air pressure makes modern machinery non-functional
  • Miners use hammers, chisels, gas drills, and sometimes illegal explosives that damage gemstones
  • Air becomes polluted with gas and dust — miners have no safety equipment (no breathing gear, air filters, or face masks)
  • Main causes of death/injury: falls from cliffs, mine collapses, falling rocks
  • Many miners suffer from lung problems and carbon monoxide poisoning
  • Gems sold in raw form very cheaply — miners do not get proper value

Mining: Economy & Environment
SECTION 05 · BENEFITS, IMPACTS & SUSTAINABILITY
Economic Benefits of Mining
  • Provides raw materials for cars, roads, bridges, railways, ships, planes, computers, phones, solar panels, medical technology, weapons, fertilisers
  • Contributes to GDP and GNP (national income indicators)
  • Countries with mineral deposits can attract foreign investment even without the means to mine themselves
  • Creates jobs and invests in human development, rural areas, research, and infrastructure
  • Finished goods produce more revenue than raw materials
Environmental Impacts of Mining

Mining activities include drilling, excavation, operation, maintenance, and expansion. Miners often abandon sites without rehabilitation (afforestation and water treatment).

Deforestation & Erosion

Large-scale deforestation and soil erosion, loss of fertile land, increased surface runoff during rainfall.

Pollution

Contamination of land, streams, and wetlands. Land, air, and water pollution throughout the process.

Biodiversity Loss

Changing biodiversity of wildlife and fishery habitats due to habitat destruction.

Climate & Health

Disturbing local climates due to global warming. Diseases like cancer, hepatitis, tuberculosis.

Sustainable Mining Methods
  • Modern methods of mining with little greenhouse emissions
  • Treatment plants for disposal of waste
  • Protective gear for miners
  • Rehabilitation of land after mining
  • Strict implementation of environmental laws to avoid air and water pollution
  • Using the 3Rs: Reducing wastage, Reusing resources, Recycling scrap metals
  • Use of alternative renewable substitutes
  • Use of mineral resources in a sustainable manner
⚠️
Safety in Pakistan's Mines (2016 Report)

Industrial Global Union reported poor safety and working conditions. Most mines lack nearby medical help. From 2010–May 2019, at least 414 coal miners were killed in ~93 accidents; at least 104 mine workers died in 2018 alone.

Fossil Fuels
SECTION 06 · COAL, OIL & GAS
DEFINITION

A fossil fuel is a non-renewable resource formed naturally over millions of years from dead trees and plants. A fossil fuel power station generates thermal energy by burning fossil fuel. This heat turns water to steam, which moves turbines (mechanical energy) to generate electricity. Since the 18th century coal-fired steam engines, fossil fuel use has steadily increased.

🌏
Global Fact

Globally, humans now burn over 4,000 times more fossil fuels than in 1776.

✅ Advantages of Fossil Fuels
  • Cheap source of energy
  • Efficient — produces more energy than renewables
  • Easy to transport
  • Useful by-products (plastics, fuel, etc.)
❌ Disadvantages of Fossil Fuels
  • Limited reserves (non-renewable)
  • Extraction leads to soil erosion
  • Increases the rate of global warming
  • Few deposits lead to imports & dependent economy

Coal — Formation & Mining
SECTION 07 · TYPES, FORMATION & METHODS
What is Coal?

Coal is a fossil fuel containing mainly carbon. When dead plant material is converted into coal, the process is called carbonisation. Pakistan produced over 7 million tonnes of coal and has 175 billion tonnes in reserves (Geological Survey of Pakistan).

How Coal is Formed
1
Plants DieTrees and plants died millions of years ago and sank deeper underground.
2
Peat Bog FormsMud and water covered plant material, trapping carbon → forming great peat bogs.
3
Pressure & Heat → LigniteHigh pressure and high temperature converted some peat bogs into lignite (brown coal).
4
Deeper Burial → Sub-bituminousFurther burial under sediment increased pressure and heat → lignite became sub-bituminous coal.
5
Bituminous CoalMore pressure and heat turned sub-bituminous into bituminous coal (the most common type).
6
Anthracite & GraphiteExtreme pressure turned some deposits into anthracite; a few became graphite.

Coal in Pakistan

About 98% of Pakistan's coal is in Sindh, mainly in the Thar coalfield. Smaller coalfields exist in Punjab and Balochistan. Small production also in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. Coal has been mined here since the 1880s. Pakistan mainly has low-grade coal (lignite and sub-bituminous), but has some high-grade bituminous reserves.

Coal provides less than 1% of Pakistan's electricity. Only 0.2% of Pakistan's workforce was employed in mining/quarrying in 2014.

Mining Methods

Pakistan's coal is mainly from deep mining. There are two types:

Longwall Mining

Most common in Pakistan. A machine cuts coal from the coalface (100–350 m long). Hydraulic supports hold the roof while coal is cut, then the roof is allowed to collapse section by section. Enables over 75% of coal to be mined. In practice, many Pakistani mines still use pickaxes and wooden pit props.

Room and Pillar

Miners cut 'rooms' into the coal seam and leave 'pillars' of coal to support the roof. Pillars use up to 40% of total coal. Used at Lakhra mines, Sindh.

Surface Mining (Open Pit / Open Cast)

Used when coal is near the surface. Explosives break up soil/rock, then machines/hand tools drill and break the coal. Loaded onto trucks or conveyors. Covers many km². Land should be restored after mining. Destroys landscapes, forests, and wildlife habitats.

Crude Oil & Natural Gas
SECTION 08 · LIQUID & GAS FOSSIL FUELS
Crude Oil

Crude oil is a viscous, inflammable, liquid fossil fuel with many hydrocarbons. It is the most valuable and wanted fuel in the world. Treated crude oil can be used as:

USEGasoline (petrol) for cars
USEDiesel for trucks, trains, ships
USEKerosene fuel for jets
USELubricants
USEManufacturing plastics
USEMedicine & cosmetics

Extracted by drilling through rocks on land or offshore on the continental shelf. Measured in barrels (= 42 US gallons / 159 litres).

🌍
Top Oil Producers (NASDAQ)

USA, Saudi Arabia, Russia, Canada, China — together provide 54% of global output. Pakistan has very little crude oil and is dependent on imports for transportation, industrial, and power needs.

⚠️
Pollution Fact

1 litre of oil can contaminate 1 million litres of water by spreading over its surface in a thin layer that stops oxygen reaching plants and animals.

Natural Gas

Natural gas consists mostly of methane and is found near oil deposits below Earth's surface. It can be pumped through the same wells as crude oil. Used for:

  • Cooking and heating homes
  • Generating electricity
  • Raw material (feedstock) for chemicals and fertilisers

Releases 45–55% fewer greenhouse gases than coal when generating electricity. Transported by pipelines on land or by ship. The Sui Gas Field is the largest natural gas field in Pakistan — 1.6 trillion cubic feet reserve estimates (2017).

🧊
LNG — Liquefied Natural Gas

Pakistan imports LNG (natural gas cooled to liquid for safe transport). It is odorless, colorless, non-toxic, non-corrosive, and takes up about 1/600th the volume of natural gas. At the destination it is re-heated through regasification.

Alternative & Renewable Energy
SECTION 09 · SOLAR, WIND, HYDRO & MORE

Unlike thermal power, renewable energy does not use up materials to produce electricity. Energy cannot be made — only converted from one form to another. A generator uses kinetic energy to move a magnet past a coil of wire, pushing electrons through the coil to create electricity.

💧
RENEWABLE
Hydro-Electric Power
Water flows through turbines in dams, pushing blades to spin a generator. Pakistan has 80+ hydro power stations generating ~10,000 megawatts. Key dams: Tarbela, Warsak, Mangla (Azad Kashmir), Chasma (Punjab). Once 45% of Pakistan's electricity; now 28% (1991). Pakistan ranked 3rd in new hydro capacity additions in 2018.
☀️
RENEWABLE
Solar Power
PV (photovoltaic) cells absorb sunlight to start electrical charges — no generator needed. Pakistan has ~8.5 hours of sunlight per day. First commercial solar plant completed 2015; by 2017 there were 6 plants (4 more due by 2022). Together generating 430 megawatts. Works on cloudy days too.
💨
RENEWABLE
Wind Power
Wind turbines generate electricity. Must be in windy locations (e.g. near coasts). Pakistan has wind plants at Jhimper and Gharo in Thatta district, Sindh. Developing more in coastal areas of Sindh and Balochistan.
🌊
POTENTIAL
Tidal Power
Harnesses moving water from tides in the sea. Pakistan has not yet developed tidal power but some sites are being explored off the coast of Sindh and Balochistan.
🌋
RENEWABLE
Geothermal Energy
Heat from below Earth's surface. The deeper you dig, the hotter it gets. Useful near tectonic activity (volcanoes, plate boundaries). Pakistan has no geothermal plants yet, but potential where Indian Plate meets Eurasian Plate. Geothermal energy in hot springs, e.g. Garam Chasma, Chitral, KPK.
🌿
RENEWABLE
Biomass Energy
Comes from living/once-living organisms (plants, wood, corn, soy, food/animal waste). Burns to create heat for electricity — similar to coal, oil, and gas. Carbon-neutral if managed sustainably.
⚛️
NON-RENEWABLE
Nuclear Power
Nuclear fission splits uranium atoms to heat water → steam → turbines. Does NOT produce greenhouse gases but produces radioactive waste. Pakistan uses nuclear power stations and has uranium deposits mainly in central and southern parts of the country.
✅ Advantages of Renewable Energy
  • Can be set up in remote areas
  • Independent economy
  • Less greenhouse emissions
  • Inexhaustible resource
❌ Disadvantages of Renewable Energy
  • Expensive set-up and maintenance
  • Loss of land that can be farmed
  • Unreliable (dependent on tides, clouds, wind)
  • Disturbs ecosystems
Economic Dependence on Energy

Energy usage is divided into four sectors: Residential, Commercial, Transportation, Industrial. All sectors of agriculture and the economy depend on energy supply.

Countries with energy shortages face load shedding and rely on fuel imports. This causes manufacturing delays, business losses, unemployment, poverty, lower GDP, and higher inflation.

🌍
Carbon Emissions Fact

IEA: Global carbon emissions in 2021 from energy combustion and industrial processes reached 36.3 gigatonnes — the highest ever annual level. China had the biggest carbon footprint in 2021, followed by USA, European Union, Japan, and Russia.

Key Terms & Exam Tips
SECTION 10 · VOCABULARY & QUICK REVISION
Mineral
Naturally occurring, inorganic solid with a chemical formula and crystal structure.
Quarrying
Extracting materials (usually rock or minerals) from an open surface pit.
Carbonisation
The process by which dead plant material is converted into coal.
Turbine
A machine with rotating blades that converts energy (steam/water/wind) into mechanical energy to run a generator.
Thermal Energy
Heat energy — produced when fossil fuels are burned in a power station.
Fossil Fuel
Non-renewable resource (coal, oil, natural gas) formed from ancient dead organisms over millions of years.
GDP
Gross Domestic Product — measures economic productivity within a country's borders.
GNP
Gross National Product — measures economic activity by a country's citizens even outside its borders.
Load Shedding
Deliberate shutdown of electricity supply to reduce load on the electricity grid during shortages.
LNG
Liquefied Natural Gas — natural gas cooled to liquid for transport; converted back through regasification.
Longwall Mining
Deep mining method where a machine cuts coal from a coalface 100–350 m long; roof collapses after.
Regasification
Process of re-heating LNG at its destination to convert it back into gas.
Lubricant
A substance (e.g. powdered graphite) used to reduce friction between moving parts.
Nuclear Fission
Splitting atoms (usually uranium) apart to form smaller atoms, releasing energy.
Gemstone
A piece of mineral crystal that can be cut and polished for jewellery or ornaments.
Photovoltaic (PV)
Solar panels made of PV cells that absorb sunlight and convert it to electrical energy.
